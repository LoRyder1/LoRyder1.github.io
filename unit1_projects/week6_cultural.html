Week #6:

Stereotype Threat - Claude M. Steele

There is underperformance of groups when they are stereotyped. Diversity problem- one thing to integrate numerically, another where everyone feels they can flourish. Social Identity - our sense of who we are. If you have to deal with things because of your identity then it affects your sense of identity- Identity contigencies. It becomes who you are, it structures the way you think. Identity is very situational.

"People often see themselves in term of whichever one of their allegiances (identities) is under attack..." - Amin Maalouf

Being in a situation where the stereotype can be applied to you = stereotype threat. Frustration is the ingredient that makes the stereotype active in performance. Caring makes you the most vulnerable to the identity contigencies. Very little subjective sense of the threat: everyone will deny it. Underperforming because your trying too hard - it makes you the most vulnerable.

Bad news: Identity is intrinsic to most diverse settings, the default state of affairs unless something is done to reduce it.

How to mitigate the Stereotype Threat

Identity Security - give a sense of belonging, and reasurance. Strategies for Identity safety: leadership, critical mass, resist permanent tracking, deracializing perception of setting, self-affirmation, belongingness interventions, challenge and support, relationships, wise teaching.

The old axiom that "ignorance is bliss" hold true with stereotypes - the more you believe in them, the more they may be true for you. Stereotypes box us into unconscious expectations for ourselves (and often, others too).
